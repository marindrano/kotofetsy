# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Ikotofetsy serves two primary audiences.

Recruiters and developers visit the public site to evaluate the author's ability to ship a full-stack AI product: crawling, indexing, backend APIs, retrieval, AI synthesis, and a polished frontend. They should understand the technical depth quickly without needing to read a long portfolio essay.

News-light readers visit the product to understand Malagasy and Madagascar-related news without following every outlet. They need fast search, plain-language summaries, cross-outlet comparison, and story history that helps them catch up without feeling buried.

Authenticated users can return to deeper workflows: asking the AI follow-up questions, bookmarking stories, saving searches, keeping reading history, and tracking topics over time.

## Product Purpose

Ikotofetsy is a Malagasy news intelligence product. It crawls Malagasy and Madagascar news sources, indexes articles, and uses AI to summarize stories, compare coverage across outlets, surface bias-aware synthesis, and trace how a story evolved over time.

The public site should make the product immediately legible as a serious AI/full-stack system while still feeling specifically Malagasy rather than a generic AI wrapper. The product should help people who do not usually read or hear news get the balanced version quickly.

Success means a visitor can understand the product promise, inspect or try a credible news experience, and see that the system is technically real: data acquisition, search, storage, AI reasoning, and UI working together.

## Brand Personality

Serious, clever, grounded.

The brand should carry the Ikotofetsy folklore reference as a quiet strategic idea: resourceful intelligence cutting through noise. It should not become cartoonish folklore decoration. The public surface should feel trustworthy enough for news, sharp enough for AI, and locally specific enough for the Malagasy media context.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic AI SaaS pages with abstract gradients, vague productivity claims, and no proof of product depth.

Avoid the default React/TanStack starter identity. The current React atom logo is a placeholder signal, not the Ikotofetsy brand.

Avoid newspaper-portal clutter: dense ad-like grids, too many headlines, weak hierarchy, and visual noise that makes news feel tiring.

Avoid dark hacker dashboards as the main public identity. Technical credibility should come from working product evidence, not terminal cosplay.

Avoid portfolio pages that mostly describe the builder. The product should demonstrate the builder's skill through real workflows, architecture cues, and interaction quality.

Avoid civic/NGO blandness. The product can serve public understanding without looking bureaucratic or generic.

## Design Principles

Lead with the working product, not the resume. Recruiters should infer competence from concrete product surfaces, architecture proof, and AI workflows.

Make news feel catch-up friendly. The interface should reduce anxiety for people who do not follow news closely: clear summaries, source comparison, timelines, and next-step questions.

Use cleverness as structure, not decoration. The Ikotofetsy idea should show up through comparison, synthesis, and smart navigation more than literal folklore imagery.

Show source plurality. Trust depends on making outlets, dates, and coverage differences visible instead of hiding them behind a single AI answer.

Keep public and app surfaces distinct. Public SEO pages can be more expressive and narrative; `/app` or `/studio` should be quieter, denser, and optimized for repeated search and reading workflows.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and interactive controls.

Design for Malagasy, French, and English content lengths where possible. Labels and layouts should tolerate longer translated strings.

Support reduced motion. Motion may help public storytelling, but product workflows should remain fast and state-driven.

Do not rely on color alone to communicate outlet stance, status, source type, or AI confidence.
